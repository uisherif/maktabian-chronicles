The Maktabian
Chronicles

A Family Saga of Transformation and Survival

By Sherif Maktabi & Claude

Scroll

From Zoroastrian fire priests in Safavid Isfahan to Muslim merchants in Ottoman Baghdad to cosmopolitan traders in French Mandate Beirut—one family survives by transforming, changing everything except their stubborn refusal to disappear.

Span
475 years across three empires
Journey
Isfahan → Baghdad → Beirut
Transformations
Four religions, countless identities, one family
Magic
A prophetic carpet woven in 1488, mapping past and future

This is a story about identity as continuity rather than consistency. About home as story rather than place. About survival as transformation rather than preservation. And about the magic that emerges when a family refuses to forget who they are, even as they become something entirely new.
